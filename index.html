01 · Overview

Purpose & Framework

This tool reframes the football operations audit model for the realities of Track & Field and Cross Country — large rosters, dual indoor/outdoor seasons, smaller travel parties across more meets, hosted-meet operations, and event-specific equipment cycles. Use it to track expenditures, monitor compliance, identify risk, and support data-driven decisions.

Phase 1

Data Collection

Pull from cost center reports, purchasing transactions, travel/meet expenditures, recruiting budgets, vendor contracts, and equipment purchases. Sources: Business Office, accounting systems, coaching operational reports.

Phase 2

Financial Analysis

Compute budget variance, cost per athlete, cost per meet, recruiting cost per signee, travel cost efficiency, equipment lifecycle cost, and hosted-meet net cost.

Phase 3

Compliance Review

Layer NCAA Bylaw 16 (or NAIA equivalents), recruiting financial standards, transportation policy, booster/NIL disclosure, and institutional policy compliance into every financial line.

Strategic Value

Why this fits a coordinator-level role

The role of Coordinator of Track & Field Operations, Compliance & Student-Athlete Development is the financial-and-compliance nerve center of a program. This tool consolidates the responsibilities the position exists to discharge — budget oversight, compliance documentation, operational reporting, and cross-department coordination — into one defensible workflow. The output is the report you hand the AD, Business Office, and Head Coach without scrambling.
